    \abstract{
    Photonic quantum technologies rely on the efficient generation and interference of indistinguishable photons.
    Exceptional achievements in this respect have been obtained by domain engineering of quasi-phase-matched spontaneous parametric down-conversion (SPDC) sources, demonstrating high two-photon interference visibility using only moderate bandpass spectral filtering [1].
    Here, we optimised the spectral purity and indistinguishability of photons from telecom-wavelength sources by combining Gaussian quasi-phase-matching with Gaussian pump spectral shaping.
    Without spectral filtering, we used time-of-flight spectrometry to estimate an upper bound spectral purity of 99.9272(6)\%, and achieved visibilities of up to 98.5(8)\% in two-photon interference experiments with independent sources.
    }

    \block{l}{Hong-Ou-Mandel interference and indistinguishability}{
        In photonic quantum technologies, the lack of direct photon interaction is compensated for by exploiting two-particle interference: the so-called Hong-Ou-Mandel (HOM) effect [2].
        High visibility of this interference requires high indistinguishability in all internal degrees of freedom, including relative delay (see figure below), polarisation, and frequency.\\[0.25cm]

    \block{r}{Joint Spectral Amplitude (JSA) optimisation}{
        The bi-photon wave function $\ket{\psi}_{s,i}=\iint \mathrm{d}\omega_s \mathrm{d}\omega_i f(\omega_s,\omega_i) \hat{a}^{\dagger}_s(\omega_s)\hat{a}^{\dagger}_i(\omega_i)\ket{0}$ depends on the JSA $f(\omega_s,\omega_i)=\phi(\omega_s,\omega_i)\alpha(\omega_s+\omega_i)$. This directly relates to energy and momentum conservation:\\[0.25cm]
        \begin{itemize}
            \item energy conservation $\Rightarrow$ Pump Envelope Function (PEF): $\alpha(\omega_p)=\alpha(\omega_s + \omega_i)$
            \item momentum conservation $\Rightarrow$ Phase-Matching Function (PMF): $\phi(\omega_s, \omega_i)=\int \chi^{(2)}(z)e^{i\Delta k(\omega_i,\omega_s)z}\mathrm{d}z$
        \end{itemize}
        \vspace{0.5cm}
        \begin{minipage}[b]{0.5\textwidth}
            {\centering
            \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{pics/Purity_v8_labels.pdf}}
        \end{minipage}
        \raisebox{-2ex}{
        \begin{minipage}[b]{0.5\textwidth}
            {\centering
            \includegraphics[width=.5\textwidth]{pics/ppKTP.png}\\[0.5cm]
            \hspace{3cm}
            \includegraphics[width=.6\textwidth]{pics/ppKTP_interference.pdf}}\\[0.25cm]
            {
            $\Rightarrow$ Standard phase-matching and quasi-phase-matching: constant nonlinearity profile leading to sinc-shaped PMF with side-lobes.\\[0.25cm]
            $\Rightarrow$ Usual picosecond and femtosecond pump lasers: hyperbolic secant spectral shape increasing spectral correlations.\\[0.25cm]
            $\Rightarrow$ Gaussian shaping of both the PMF and the PEF leads to an optimised JSA without spectral correlations [3].
            }
        \end{minipage}}
        
    }
